## Projects Killed By Google

<ProjectCard title="Google Chrome Apps" link="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Chrome_App" bg="linear-gradient(to right, #D4145A 0%, #FBB03B 100%)">
Fading into darkness in over 1 year, Google Chrome Apps were hosted or packaged web applications that ran on the Google Chrome browser. It will be over 11 years old.
</ProjectCard>

<ProjectCard title="Android Things" link="https://android-developers.googleblog.com/2019/02/an-update-on-android-things.html" bg="linear-gradient(to right, #D4145A 0%, #FBB03B 100%)">
Running out of juice in 10 months, Android Things was an Android-based embedded operating system (originally named Brillo) aimed to run on Internet of Things (IoT) devices. It will be over 6 years old.
</ProjectCard>

<ProjectCard title="AngularJS" link="https://blog.angular.io/stable-angularjs-and-long-term-support-7e077635ee9c" bg="linear-gradient(to right, #D4145A 0%, #FBB03B 100%)">
Biting the big one in 10 months, AngularJS was a JavaScript open-source front-end web framework based on MVC pattern using a dependency injection technique. It will be about 11 years old.
</ProjectCard>

<ProjectCard title="Google Hangouts" link="https://arstechnica.com/gadgets/2019/01/the-great-google-hangouts-shutdown-begins-october-2019/" bg="linear-gradient(to right, #D4145A 0%, #FBB03B 100%)">
Floating belly up in 4 months, Google Hangouts was a communication platform which included messages, video chat, and VOIP features. Execution scheduled "first half 2021". It will be over 8 years old.
</ProjectCard>

<ProjectCard title="Expeditions" link="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Expeditions" bg="linear-gradient(to right, #D4145A 0%, #FBB03B 100%)">
Another one bites the dust in 4 months, Expeditions is a program for providing virtual reality experiences to school classrooms through Google Cardboard viewers, allowing educators to take their students on virtual field trips. It will be almost 6 years old.
</ProjectCard>
